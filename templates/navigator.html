Law & Procedure Navigator (India)

Simplified step-by-step guides for common legal processes in India.

How to File a First Information Report (FIR)

  1. Go to the nearest police station that has jurisdiction over the area where the crime was committed.
  2. Inform the officer on duty about the offense, either orally or in writing.
  3. The officer will write down the information. It must be read over to you.
  4. Once verified, you must sign the FIR.
  5. You are entitled to a free copy of the FIR. If the police refuse to register it, you can send a written complaint to the Superintendent of Police (SP).

Official Portal: Varies by state (e.g., check your state police website).

How to File a Consumer Complaint Online

  1. Visit the official e-Daakhil portal: edaakhil.nic.in
  2. Register as a new user and create an account.
  3. Fill out the complaint form with details of the consumer, the opposing party, and the nature of the complaint.
  4. Attach supporting documents (bills, receipts, warranty cards, etc.).
  5. Pay the required fee online. The fee depends on the value of the claim.
  6. The complaint will be processed by the relevant consumer commission.

Official Portal: e-Daakhil Portal

Basic Company Registration Process (Pvt. Ltd.)

  1. Obtain a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for the proposed directors.
  2. Apply for the Director Identification Number (DIN).
  3. Apply for name reservation using the SPICe+ Part A form on the MCA portal.
  4. Draft the Memorandum of Association (MoA) and Articles of Association (AoA).
  5. Fill and submit the SPICe+ Part B form along with all required documents.
  6. The Registrar of Companies (ROC) will verify the documents and issue the Certificate of Incorporation.

Official Portal: Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)
